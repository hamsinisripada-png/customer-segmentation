# Customer Segmentation using Machine Learning

## Overview

In today's world customer is indeed the king and hence it is not only important to retain our loyal customers but also to win back those who are on the verge of churning out. Customer Segmentation is one method to identify who our loyal customers are and who are our one-time visitors.

This project demonstrates how to perform Customer Segmentation using Machine Learning techniques such as RFM Analysis and K-Means Clustering.

---

## Customer Segmentation

Customer Segmentation is a methodology using which we can divide our customer base into groups of individuals who are similar in terms of:

- Spending behavior
- Frequency of purchases
- Age
- Gender
- Other demographic characteristics

Customer Segmentation allows companies to precisely target customers with specific needs and preferences. This helps businesses design targeted campaigns for the right audience. During segmentation, companies may also identify new market opportunities and customer groups requiring immediate attention, especially those at risk of churn.

---

## About Data

This dataset has been retrieved from Kaggle.

The **Online Retail II Dataset** contains all transactions occurring for a UK-based and registered non-store online retail business between:

**01/12/2009 and 09/12/2011**

---

## Data Preparation

As a first step, all required modules were imported and the dataset was loaded.

### Country Distribution Analysis

The country column contains customer records from different countries.

After analyzing the distribution, it was observed that approximately **90% of customers belong to the United Kingdom**. Therefore, only UK customers were retained for further analysis.

### Missing Value Analysis

The dataset contains missing values in:

- Description
- CustomerID

Missing values in the Description column were ignored, while CustomerID records with missing values were removed during preprocessing.

---

## Data Analysis

### Recency Analysis

**Recency** indicates how recently a customer has made a purchase.

The more recently a customer has made a purchase, the more likely they are to continue engaging with the business in the future.

---

### Frequency Analysis

**Frequency** represents the number of purchases made by a customer during their lifetime.

Customers who purchase more frequently are generally more likely to make future purchases compared to customers who purchase only once or infrequently.

---

### Monetary Analysis

**Monetary Value** represents the total revenue generated by a customer.

Customers with higher spending are typically more valuable to the business and are more likely to continue purchasing in the future.

---

## K-Means Clustering

To perform customer segmentation using Machine Learning, K-Means Clustering was applied.

### Elbow Method

The Elbow Method was used to determine the optimal number of clusters.

The Sum of Squared Errors (SSE) decreases significantly until **K = 3**, indicating that:

```text
Optimal Number of Clusters = 3
```

Therefore, the number of clusters selected for this project is three.
